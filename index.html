Vietnamese Culture

History

There is long history of occupation in Vietnam including the occupation by several Chinese dynasties. This includes the rule of feudal systems under Confucius ideology.

There was also the colonization of Vietnam from countries like France and Japan. During the 1800s, the French briefly colonized Vietnam, which would later have lasting impacts. And during World War II, Japan invaded Vietnam.

But in the 1950s, Ho Chi Minh became the president of Vietnam and worked towards the liberation of Northern Vietnam as the communist party grew.

Cuisine

Common spices: lemongrass, ginger, mint, cinnamon, chili, and basil

There are an endless amount of exotics spices in Vietnamese cuisine. Some are difficult to come by in the United States.

Dishes like phở, bánh mì, and vermicelli bowls can be commonly found in the United States.

After the Vietnam War, many Vietnamese refugees fled to America and started small businesses to provide for their families. Many of these businesses include nail salons and Vietnamese restaurants.

Wardrobe

There are a vast amount of ethnicities in Vietnam, each whom have distinct traditional clothing. Amongst these ethnic groups, include the Hmong people.

Vibrant colors and headpieces are common themes in traditional attire. Áo dàis are common dresses for celebratory events; these dresses can be commonly seen at weddings for both men and women.

Weddings

Weddings can be very large celebrations, complete with hundreds of people. It is not uncommon for the bride and groom to not know many of their guests.

Traditions can involve large gift givings and other ceremonies like tea services, candle lightings, and various blessings. Commonly, the bride wears red and the groom wears blue.
